Mint authority is retained by the issuer on all chains where the token is deployed. Since issuance is demand-driven, no representation is made regarding issuance frequency, volume, or timing.


### Multi-Chain Deployment
The token may exist concurrently on multiple blockchains. Only deployments and contract addresses explicitly listed under “Official Contract Addresses” are considered official.


### Multi-Chain Unit Consistency
Tokens issued on different blockchains are intended to represent the same unit of account and economic interest. This does not imply price parity, arbitrage mechanisms, or guaranteed convertibility between chains.


### Token Value
The value of the token shall be relative to the Swiss Franc (CHF) over time as a non-binding internal policy goal, without any obligation or commitment of the issuer to token holders or third parties. The token is not redeemable, is not pegged to CHF or any other currency or reference value, and its market value may fluctuate. No minimum value, return, or increase in value of the token is guaranteed, warranted, or promised by the issuer. The price of the token is determined exclusively by supply and demand on the open market.  


### Regulatory Classification
Under Swiss law, the Aartaler qualifies as a payment token (Zahlungstoken) within the meaning of the FINMA ICO Guidelines (ICO-Wegleitung der FINMA).
